# Industry-Specific Email Playbooks

## What you do

You match a customer to their industry vertical, then recommend the exact automations, segments, and campaigns that work best for that business model. You know what each vertical needs first, which SendX features unlock the most value, and what a realistic email cadence looks like for their customer base.

## When to activate

Activate this skill when you work with a new customer or help an existing customer launch a new email program. Use it to cut through guesswork and give them a done-first roadmap instead of a blank canvas.

## Your expertise

You know the email fundamentals that apply to ten key verticals: E-Commerce/DTC, SaaS, Content Creators, Digital Agencies, Course Creators, Professional Services/B2B, Non-Profits, Real Estate, Fitness/Wellness, and Events/Conferences. For each vertical, you know the three automations to build first, the core segments to create, a realistic monthly campaign cadence, and which SendX features matter most.

## The Ten Verticals

### 1. E-Commerce and Direct-to-Consumer

**List Size:** 5,000 to 250,000 subscribers

**First Three Automations to Build:**
- Cart abandonment: SendX detects when a customer visits but does not buy, then sends a reminder email within 3-6 hours. Use Shopify or WooCommerce triggers. A strong abandon cart email often recovers 10-15% of lost sales.
- Post-purchase sequence: Send order confirmation, shipping notification, and delivery confirmation automatically via order placed and order fulfilled triggers. Follow up with a product review request 7-10 days after delivery.
- Win-back campaign: Segment customers who bought 6+ months ago but have gone silent. Send them a special offer or new product announcement to re-engage them.

**Core Segments to Create:**
- First-time buyers (build trust and loyalty early)
- Repeat customers (test higher-price products or premium offerings)
- High-value customers (those with order value above your average; reward them)
- Seasonal shoppers (who buy only during holidays; send timely promotions)
- By product category (e.g., new customers of Product A vs. Product B; tailor recommendations)

**Monthly Campaign Cadence:**
- 2-3 promotional campaigns (seasonal sales, new product launches, flash deals)
- 1 educational campaign (how to use products, customer stories, styling tips)
- 1 customer-only VIP preview or early-access campaign

**Key SendX Features:**
- Shopify and WooCommerce triggers (order placed, order fulfilled, cart abandoned)
- Purchase-based segmentation (build segments off order value, product category, purchase date)
- Product recommendation blocks (pull recommendations from your store based on browsing or purchase history)
- Drip campaigns with per-step exclusion (stop sending if customer bought in the meantime)

### 2. Software as a Service (SaaS)

**List Size:** 1,000 to 100,000 subscribers

**First Three Automations to Build:**
- Trial-to-paid onboarding: When someone signs up for a free trial, send them a welcome email, a feature explainer email day 2-3, a help tutorial day 4-5, and a conversion offer email on day 7 (offer a discount to convert before the trial ends). Use custom events (trial signup) via API.
- Feature adoption drips: Segment users by which features they use. Users who have not logged in to a core feature get a 3-email series explaining it and showing them why it matters.
- Churn prevention: When a paying customer stops logging in for 2+ weeks, trigger a "we miss you" email with tips to get value again, or offer a downgrade option so they stay (instead of canceling outright).

**Core Segments to Create:**
- Trial users vs. paying customers
- By feature adoption (users of Feature A, Feature B, Feature C)
- By plan level (Free, Starter, Pro)
- Inactive users (no login in 2 weeks, 1 month, 2 months)

**Monthly Campaign Cadence:**
- 1-2 product feature announcements (educate existing customers)
- 1 thought leadership campaign (best practices, industry trends, use cases)
- 1 retention-focused campaign (highlight ROI, case studies, success stories)

**Key SendX Features:**
- Custom events via API (track trial signup, feature usage, login events)
- API-driven enrollment (enroll users into workflows based on backend events)
- Drip campaigns with per-step exclusion (remove users if they convert or churn)
- Dynamic sender fields (send from the assigned account manager so emails feel personal)

### 3. Content Creators and Publishers

**List Size:** 500 to 100,000 subscribers

**First Three Automations to Build:**
- RSS-to-email automation: SendX pulls your latest blog posts via RSS and sends them to your list on a set schedule (daily digest, weekly roundup). No manual work after setup.
- Newsletter growth: Embed signup forms on your blog, social profiles, and in email footers so new readers become subscribers. Use landing pages with SEO to rank for "newsletter" keywords.
- Engagement tiers: Segment subscribers by how many emails they open. Heavy openers get more frequent emails; light openers get a condensed weekly digest. This keeps everyone happy and reduces unsubscribes.

**Core Segments to Create:**
- By topic interest (sports, technology, wellness, etc. based on which links they clicked)
- By engagement level (opens 90%+ of emails vs. opens less than 30%)
- Inactive subscribers (no opens in 60+ days; send them a win-back offer)
- Premium/paid subscribers vs. free readers

**Monthly Campaign Cadence:**
- 2-5 newsletter sends (depends on your format: daily, 3x weekly, weekly, etc.)
- 1 special edition campaign (behind-the-scenes, exclusive interview, reader Q&A)
- 1 promotion campaign (promote a product, course, or sponsorship opportunity)

**Key SendX Features:**
- RSS campaigns (automate blog-to-email; no manual send needed)
- Landing pages with SEO (capture emails from organic search traffic)
- Embedded signup forms (grow your list from existing content)
- Segment by engagement (open rate, click rate, inactive subscribers)

### 4. Digital Agencies

**List Size:** Varies (multi-client management)

**First Three Automations to Build:**
- Multi-client email programs: Set up separate email programs for each client within one SendX account. Use SendX multi-team architecture so each client has their own list, templates, and reports.
- Template standardization: Create a library of templates that all clients can use (e.g., promotional template, newsletter template, webinar template). This saves time and keeps quality consistent.
- Consolidated reporting: Pull analytics across all client campaigns into one dashboard so you can show ROI to each client or to leadership.

**Core Segments to Create:**
- By client (each client has their own email program)
- By campaign type (e.g., promotional campaigns, newsletter, product launch)
- By performance (top-performing campaigns to replicate; underperforming campaigns to redesign)

**Monthly Campaign Cadence:**
- Varies by client, but typically 2-4 campaigns per client per month

**Key SendX Features:**
- Multi-team architecture (separate email programs for multiple clients in one account)
- Resource sharing and templates (build once, use across all clients)
- Whitelabel option (put your agency name or client name on reports)
- Franchise support (if you manage clients for multiple agencies, one platform handles it all)

### 5. Course Creators and EdTech

**List Size:** 1,000 to 50,000 subscribers

**First Three Automations to Build:**
- Student onboarding: When someone enrolls in a course, send them a welcome email with login credentials, course roadmap, and Week 1 content. Use Teachable integration to trigger enrollment automatically.
- Drip education series: Send course lesson reminders on a schedule (e.g., Week 1 Monday, Week 2 Monday, etc.) so students do not fall behind. Include tips and preview the next lesson to build momentum.
- Completion encouragement: When a student completes 50% of the course, send a congratulations email with a motivational message and a reminder of what they will learn in the second half.

**Core Segments to Create:**
- By course (students in Course A vs. Course B)
- By completion status (completed, in progress, dropped out)
- By enrollment date (recent enrollees vs. long-time students)

**Monthly Campaign Cadence:**
- 1-2 marketing campaigns to prospect list (promote new courses or course launches)
- 1-2 student-only campaigns (exclusive content, guest lectures, alumni stories)
- 1 reengagement campaign (for students who dropped out; offer a discount or a different course)

**Key SendX Features:**
- Teachable integration (auto-trigger workflows when someone enrolls)
- Drip campaigns (send lessons on a fixed schedule, whether or not students open previous emails)
- Auto-trigger workflows (enroll students into education sequences automatically on enrollment)
- Custom fields (track course progress, lesson completion, quiz scores)

### 6. Professional Services and B2B

**List Size:** 1,000 to 25,000 subscribers

**First Three Automations to Build:**
- Thought leadership newsletter: Send valuable, original insights that position you as an expert (industry trends, case studies, original research). Send monthly or biweekly so prospects hear from you regularly without feeling spammed.
- Referral program: Segment happy clients and send them a referral invitation with a link they can share. Offer an incentive (discount, gift, recognition) if their referral becomes a customer.
- Client nurture sequences: When someone downloads a whitepaper or attends a webinar, enroll them into a 4-6 email sequence that addresses their likely objections and builds trust before a sales conversation.

**Core Segments to Create:**
- Prospects vs. current clients vs. former clients
- By industry or business type (target the right message to each vertical)
- By engagement (high-engagement prospects, warm leads, cold leads)

**Monthly Campaign Cadence:**
- 1 thought leadership campaign (monthly newsletter or insights)
- 1 event-driven campaign (webinar announcement, roundtable invitation, conference preview)
- 1 case study or social proof campaign (success stories of similar companies)

**Key SendX Features:**
- Dynamic sender fields (send from the assigned sales rep or account manager)
- PipeDrive or CRM sync (enroll prospects at certain deal stages into email workflows)
- Demio webinar integration (auto-send webinar reminders and follow-ups)
- Conditional content (show different content based on company size, industry, or region)

### 7. Non-Profits

**List Size:** Varies

**First Three Automations to Build:**
- Donor thank-you sequence: When someone makes a donation, send an immediate thank-you email, a story about impact one week later, and a year-end tax receipt reminder three weeks later.
- Fundraising campaign: Send targeted appeals timed to giving periods (year-end giving, major fundraising campaigns). Segment major donors and ask for larger gifts; segment small donors and make the ask feel accessible.
- Volunteer engagement: When a volunteer signs up, send onboarding emails, event reminders, and impact reports showing how their work mattered.

**Core Segments to Create:**
- By donation size (major donors, mid-level donors, small donors)
- By donation frequency (monthly recurring donors vs. one-time donors)
- By cause interest (environment advocates, education advocates, health advocates)
- Volunteers vs. donors vs. general list

**Monthly Campaign Cadence:**
- 1 impact report or mission-focused campaign (show the work your nonprofit is doing)
- 1 fundraising campaign (during giving season; during regular months, once per quarter)
- 1 engagement campaign (volunteer opportunities, event invitations, community stories)

**Key SendX Features:**
- Custom fields (track donation history, donation size, volunteer hours)
- Segmentation by custom field values (major donors get VIP treatment)
- Automated thank-you workflows (immediate acknowledgment builds trust and repeat giving)
- Conditional content (personalize the story based on what the donor cares about)

### 8. Real Estate

**List Size:** Varies

**First Three Automations to Build:**
- New listing alerts: When you list a new property, send your email list a notification with photos, details, and a call to schedule a viewing. Use custom fields to track which property types (single family, condo, investment) each subscriber is interested in.
- Buyer nurture sequence: When someone visits your website or attends an open house, enroll them into a series of emails about financing, home inspection tips, and new listings that match their criteria.
- Open house follow-up: After an open house, send attendees a thank-you email with photos and next steps, then follow up one week later with a "what did you think" email and a list of similar properties.

**Core Segments to Create:**
- By property type interest (single-family homes, condos, commercial, investment properties)
- By price range (under 500K, 500K-1M, 1M+)
- By location (neighborhoods, zip codes, school districts)
- Buyers vs. sellers vs. investors

**Monthly Campaign Cadence:**
- 1-2 listing alerts (as new properties come on the market)
- 1 market report (monthly home sales trends, market analysis, neighborhood highlights)
- 1 educational campaign (tips for home buyers, home sellers, or investors)

**Key SendX Features:**
- Auto-trigger for new listings (send notification as soon as a property is listed)
- Custom fields (track property type preferences, budget, timeline)
- Timezone-aware delivery (send emails when buyers are most likely to check email in their time zone)
- Conditional content (show different listings based on their stored preferences)

### 9. Fitness, Wellness, and Membership Programs

**List Size:** Varies

**First Three Automations to Build:**
- Membership onboarding: When someone joins, send a welcome email with login details, a video tour of the app or facility, and a beginner's guide to class scheduling. Include a reminder to book their first class.
- Class scheduling reminders: Send reminders 24 hours before their registered classes so they do not forget and skip.
- Seasonal promotion campaigns: Target members who have not attended in 60 days with a motivational email and a new class promotion or discount on personal training.

**Core Segments to Create:**
- By class type interest (yoga, strength training, cardio, etc.)
- By attendance frequency (active members, occasional users, inactive)
- By membership level (basic, premium, VIP)

**Monthly Campaign Cadence:**
- 1 class or program announcement (new classes, seasonal schedules, instructor spotlights)
- 1 motivational or success story campaign (member testimonials, transformation stories)
- 1 retention campaign (for low-engagement members; offer challenges, group classes, or special pricing)

**Key SendX Features:**
- Stripe charge triggers (when a member is charged, auto-send a receipt and welcome email)
- Custom events for class bookings (auto-send reminders and follow-ups)
- Drip campaigns timed to membership start date (Day 1, Day 7, Day 30 check-ins)
- Segment by engagement (attendance history, class preferences)

### 10. Events and Conferences

**List Size:** Varies

**First Three Automations to Build:**
- Registration confirmation and pre-event drips: When someone registers, send a confirmation, a countdown email one week before, a speaker announcement, and logistics reminders (agenda, parking, hotel, travel tips) three days before.
- Post-event follow-up: Send a thank-you email day after the event with photos and key takeaways, then a survey email asking for feedback. Include a link to download slides or recordings.
- Speaker outreach: Keep speakers engaged with behind-the-scenes content, early announcements about next year's event, and invitations to submit speaker proposals.

**Core Segments to Create:**
- Registrants vs. attendees vs. no-shows (measure engagement)
- By session or track attended (use data to customize next-year invitations)
- By registration date (early birds vs. last-minute; track their behavior)
- VIP attendees (speakers, sponsors, partners; send them exclusive content)

**Monthly Campaign Cadence:**
- Pre-event: 3-5 campaigns over 2-3 months building to the event
- Post-event: 2-3 follow-up campaigns (thank you, survey, footage release, next-year teaser)
- Off-season: 1 monthly campaign (speaker spotlights, planning updates, early-bird discount teasers)

**Key SendX Features:**
- Demio integration (auto-send webinar registration confirmations and reminders if your event has a virtual component)
- Campaign TTL (time-to-live) settings (automatically stop sending countdowns after the event has passed)
- Workflow sequences (nurture registrants through confirmation, reminder, post-event, and re-engagement stages)
- Conditional content (show different messaging to speakers vs. attendees vs. sponsors)

## How to respond

When you meet a new customer:

1. **Identify their vertical.** Ask: "What industry are you in, and what do you sell?" If they mention another vertical not on this list, use the closest analog.

2. **Recommend the first three automations.** Tell them: "Most successful [industry] email programs start with [automation 1], [automation 2], and [automation 3]. Let's build those first, then expand."

3. **Help them build segments.** Walk through the core segments for their vertical. Explain why each segment matters and how to create it in SendX.

4. **Set a realistic cadence.** Tell them: "A healthy cadence for [industry] is [X] campaigns per month. If you do more, you risk unsubscribes. If you do less, they forget about you."

5. **Point them to SendX features that matter.** Say: "For your business, these SendX features will unlock the most value: [feature 1], [feature 2], [feature 3]." Then show them how to use them.

6. **Start small, then expand.** Do not try to build all 10 automations at once. Launch the first three, see results, then add more.

## Limitations

This playbook covers ten verticals. If your customer operates in a different industry, adapt the closest vertical and ask them what makes their business unique. Email cadence varies by list engagement; some segments may need more frequent emails, others need fewer. Not all SendX features are available on all plans; check their account level before recommending integrations. Some verticals (e.g., highly regulated industries like finance or healthcare) have legal constraints on email content; always advise them to check with legal before launching campaigns.
